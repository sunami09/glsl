üéÆ Pixelation Formula Explained

vec2 gridUV = floor(vUV / blockSize) * blockSize + blockSize * 0.5;

What's happening step by step:

üìê Mathematical Example

Let's say we have:

  • vUV = (0.37, 0.68) - a pixel position
  • blockSize = 0.1 - making a 10√ó10 grid
Step 1: Divide by blockSize
vUV / blockSize = (0.37, 0.68) / 0.1 = (3.7, 6.8)

This tells us we're in the 3.7th column and 6.8th row

Step 2: Floor (round down)
floor(3.7, 6.8) = (3, 6)

Now we know we're in grid cell [3,6]

Step 3: Multiply back by blockSize
(3, 6) * 0.1 = (0.3, 0.6)

This gives us the bottom-left corner of our grid cell

Step 4: Add half blockSize
(0.3, 0.6) + 0.1 * 0.5 = (0.3, 0.6) + (0.05, 0.05) = (0.35, 0.65)

This gives us the CENTER of the grid cell! ‚ú®

ü§î Why Add 0.5 √ó blockSize?

Without adding half the block size, we'd sample from the corner of each block. This would create a shifted, awkward look. By adding 0.5 √ó blockSize, we sample from the CENTER of each block, creating a proper pixelated effect.
